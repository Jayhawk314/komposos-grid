KOMPOSOS Grid Map Manual

How to use the interactive grid map, what the public grid data means, how to interpret the categorical/spectral screens, and where the current findings are strong or still limited.

Fast Start

Inspect

Click a hub to inspect a balancing authority, or click a line to inspect an interchange tie. The side panel shows measured flows, demand/generation facts, queue facts, congestion evidence, and reliability/curtailment overlays where available.

Views

Geographic places BAs near representative territory centroids. Spectral places BAs by measured flow coupling, so tightly connected areas sit together and weak seams pull apart.

What-if

Click a tie to cut it, or a BA to take all its ties offline. The browser recomputes connectivity, islanding, exposed flow, queue, and known value on the disabled ties.

AI

When running locally with python -m domains.grid.agent_server, the AI tab asks the Python backend to run grounded tools. Try plain questions like where is power getting stuck? or what breaks if PJM-NYIS fails?.

What The Map Shows

UI elementMeaningHow to read it
Hub / circleA balancing authority (BA), such as PJM, MISO, CISO, NYIS, SWPP.Larger circles have more measured interchange usage with neighbors.
Line / tieMeasured direct interchange between two BAs from EIA-930 hourly telemetry.Thicker lines carry more gross interchange. Red lines are structural bottleneck screens.
Blue / amber hubsThe two sides of the spectral seam partition.This is not politics or geography. It is the weakest split found from the interchange graph.
Red tieNegative Ollivier-Ricci curvature.A thin passage between neighborhoods that are otherwise weakly connected. This is a screening signal for congestion, not a congestion dollar by itself.
Added green lineA hypothetical new tie created in What-if mode.It changes topology/connectivity only. It does not carry measured flow and is not a power-flow simulation.

Core Data Terms

TermDefinitionInterpretation
EIA-930 interchangeHourly reported power interchange between balancing authorities.The backbone of the map. It is measured telemetry, but it is BA-to-BA accounting, not physical line routing.
Gross flowSum of absolute hourly interchange over the year.How heavily a tie is used, regardless of which direction dominates.
Net flowAnnual signed flow from the net-exporting side toward the net-importing side.Useful for direction, but it can hide back-and-forth hourly usage.
Demand / net generationAnnual EIA-930 BA-level load and generation totals.Used to understand whether a region tends to import or export energy.
Queue dataLBNL interconnection queue records joined to BA/seam geography.Shows how many projects are waiting, withdrawn, or potentially relevant to a bottleneck.
Congestion spreadHourly price difference across a seam, preferably using settlement congestion components.A measured proxy for dollars trapped by congestion. Stronger than topology alone.
Same-year flowUsing 2025 flow with 2025 spread, 2024 flow with 2024 spread, etc.Prevents mixing old volumes with new price conditions.

How To Interpret The Math

Curvature

Ollivier-Ricci curvature is a structural lens on the graph. Negative curvature means the edge joins neighborhoods that do not overlap much. In grid terms, that is a candidate corridor where congestion, fragility, or single-failure exposure can concentrate.

LimitCurvature is not a direct physical overload claim and not a dollar claim. It tells you where to look next.

Fiedler / Spectral Seam

The Fiedler vector comes from the graph Laplacian. It finds the weakest global split in the measured interchange network. The map uses it both for the spectral layout and for the blue/amber seam-side colors.

Structural Similarity

The AI tool reports shared-neighbor Jaccard similarity: which BAs connect to similar neighbors. This is intentionally transparent. It is a structural screen for "acts like this BA," not an operational equivalence claim.

What-if Recompute

The UI's What-if mode recomputes connectivity in the browser. The AI server can also run a deeper Python-side Ricci/spectral recompute after cutting ties. Neither one is AC/DC load flow; they explain topology and structure, not how power would physically dispatch after a contingency.

Evidence Levels

LevelMeaningHow much to trust it
measuredDirect public measurement, such as curtailment MWh or outage customer-hours.Strong, subject to source definitions.
measured_proxyComputed from measured prices/flows, such as hourly congestion-component spread times same-year flow.Useful for value ranking; read the source and caveats.
structural_onlyGraph/math evidence without a measured dollar or price observation.Good for screening, not for spend decisions by itself.
validated_hypothesisA machine-validated candidate that still needs human/domain review.Use as a review queue, not as final truth.

Current Findings To Know

PJM-NYIS

The New York/PJM seam is a major worsening corridor. The 2025 congestion component is much larger than the 2023 baseline, and same-year flow makes it a priority study.

MISO-SWPP

The wind-belt transfer seam has strong MISO-side and SPP-side evidence. It is one of the cleanest reproducible headline numbers.

CAISO Curtailment

2023 renewable curtailment is 2.66 TWh, and most of it is local/congestion-driven. This supports the "wires problem" interpretation.

SPP Curtailment

SPP curtailment is much larger than CAISO in 2023 and mostly redispatch/congestion-driven, which replicates the wires-problem pattern outside California.

Reliability

EAGLE-I shows 1.059B customer-hours lost in 2023. Dollar valuation is huge but screening-grade because customer-class assumptions matter.

Interconnection Queue

Only 16.5% of decided LBNL queue projects complete. Withdrawn and delayed projects cluster near the same bottleneck geography.

Using The AI Tab

The AI tab is not a hosted chatbot. It works locally when the map is served by domains.grid.agent_server. The server routes natural language to grounded tools and returns cards with provenance and map actions.

Good questions:

where is power getting stuck?

what areas act like PJM?

why is PJM-NYIS important?

what breaks if PJM-NYIS fails?

what should I look at next?

what does curtailment mean? · explain the queue methodology

Highlight on map dims unrelated nodes and ties so the computed result is visible. Apply cut to map switches to What-if mode and disables the tie(s) from the AI scenario.

"Why / what does this mean" questions (explain)

Definition and methodology questions are answered by the explain tool, which retrieves verbatim passages from this repository's own committed docs (the master guide, methodology reports, waste ledger) and shows them as cards with a file § section citation. Ask it directly with python -m domains.grid.agent_tools explain what is curtailment, or just ask "why" in the AI tab and it attaches the cited passages alongside the computed answer.

This retrieval is lexical (keyword/TF-IDF), not semantic search: it surfaces passages that share words with your question, so phrase questions by keyword and treat the citation, not the ranking, as the ground truth. The excerpt is the document's words verbatim; any synthesis around it is the agent's.

Limits And Honesty Rules

This is not a physical power-flow model, not a transmission-line GIS, and not a replacement for ISO planning studies. It is a public-data screening and evidence instrument.

  • Geographic lines are schematic BA-to-BA ties, not actual routes.
  • Structural bottlenecks are candidates until joined to measured price, outage, queue, or planning evidence.
  • Some non-market seams have no public LMP on one or both sides.
  • Some BA footprint corrections still need human review.
  • Some public sources expire or change retention windows, so evidence inputs must be archived for long-term reproducibility.
