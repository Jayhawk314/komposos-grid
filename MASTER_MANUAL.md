## CHAPTER 2: CATEGORICAL STABILITY (THE AUDIT)
**Goal:** *Finding "Pipe Fights" before they cause a blowout.*

When the engine runs a **Stability Scan**, it looks for **Coherence Gaps**. In math, a gap is a failure of logic. In the grid, a gap is a **Loss of Money**.

### **Section A: The Interchange Failure (Sequential Waste)**
*   **The Math:** Path A → Path B does not equal Path B → Path A.
*   **The Reality:** This is a **Scheduling Conflict**. 
*   **The Saving:** If a facility compresses gas before it is cleaned, the filters get clogged, and energy is wasted pushing gas through dirt. The engine flags this as an "Interchange Failure." Fixing the sequence can save **$15k-$50k per year** in maintenance alone.

### **Section B: The Composition Boundary (The Barrier Leak)**
*   **The Math:** A morphism crosses a "Privilege Boundary" without a constraint.
*   **The Reality:** Energy jumping between **High-Voltage and Low-Voltage** without proper balancing.
*   **The Saving:** This gap creates "Reactive Power" (power that does no work but heats the wires). Eliminating this can reduce regional transmission losses by **2-3%**, potentially saving millions in a large grid.
